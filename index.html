Our Hawaiian Story

Aloha! Ryan fell in love with Hawaiian food after moving to O'ahu in 2004. While working on O'ahu, Maui, and the Big Island of Hawai'i, he was able to sample different dishes and was hooked . . . on katsu chicken, and especially on loco mocos.

Growing up watching his dad cook for his 14 siblings helped Ryan appreciate the joy and good memories that food brings to people. He and his father got their Certificates in Culinary Arts together in 1996. Ryan hoped to someday open a restaurant of his own.

Finally, in 2014, he'd saved up enough money to quit his job and buy a small trailer to serve Hawaiian plate lunches around Utah County. Since then, Ryan and his wife, Nancey, whom he met in Hawai'i, have been bringing local Hawaiian favorites to Utah.

In June 2025, Ryan and Nancey's dream fully came to life when they opened Good Grindz in Salem, bringing authentic island flavors, aloha spirit, and broke da mouth dishes to the community.
